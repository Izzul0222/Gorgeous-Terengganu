Keropok Lekor

Keropok lekor is a popular Malaysian snack renowned for its unique taste and texture. Originating from the state of Terengganu, this traditional delicacy is primarily made from a combination of fish, sago flour, and seasonings. The mixture is then shaped into elongated rolls before being deep-fried to achieve a crispy exterior while maintaining a chewy interior. Often served with a side of spicy dipping sauce, keropok lekor is a beloved street food that showcases the rich culinary diversity of Malaysia.

Nasi Dagang

Nasi dagang is a flavorful Malaysian dish celebrated for its aromatic rice preparation and delectable accompaniments. Originating from the east coast of Peninsular Malaysia, it features a combination of steamed glutinous rice and normal rice cooked in coconut milk, giving it a distinctive fragrance. The dish is commonly served with a side of tuna curry, pickled vegetables, and hard-boiled eggs, creating a harmonious blend of flavors and textures. Nasi dagang's popularity extends beyond breakfast, making it a sought-after meal throughout the day, cherished for its unique taste that captures the essence of Malaysian culinary diversity.

Nasi Kerabu

Nasi Kerabu is a vibrant and flavorful Malaysian dish that hails from the northeastern states, particularly Kelantan and Terengganu. The centerpiece of this dish is the blue-tinted rice, which is dyed using the petals of the butterfly pea flower, lending a visually striking aspect to the meal. The rice is typically accompanied by an array of condiments such as finely shredded herbs, vegetables, salted egg, and a variety of sambals or chili-based sauces. A protein, often grilled fish or fried chicken, is commonly added to complete this aromatic and colorful culinary ensemble, making nasi kerabu a delightful and culturally significant dish in Malaysian cuisine.

Ketupat Sotong

Ketupat sotong is a delightful Malaysian dish that features stuffed squid with a flavorful mixture of glutinous rice, herbs, and spices. The dish is known for its intricate preparation, where cleaned squid tubes are carefully filled with a stuffing composed of seasoned glutinous rice, minced meat, and aromatic ingredients. The stuffed squid is then secured and cooked to perfection, resulting in a unique combination of tender squid and savory, sticky rice. Ketupat sotong is often served during festive occasions, adding a touch of sophistication to the dining experience with its delightful blend of textures and tastes.
